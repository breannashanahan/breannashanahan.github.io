Another Headless Woman


2018
Therabands, Ciment Fondu, Wood, Steel Bar, Fiberglass
5’ x 4’ x 2’


Manuscripts from the 1400s- 1800s show incredibly detailed and artistic portrayals of the human anatomy. It is interesting to note that in almost all of these images of bodies (which begin with images of criminals; as the only subjects soiled enough with sin as to permit being used as subjects of science) to beautiful depictions of Athletes, Workers, and Patients. In all of this research however, the female body comes up for one purpose, to show the growth and development of a child during pregnancies. This singular narrative of the female body in our historical texts speaks loudly to the beginnings of voyeurism of the female body and our present suppositions on the female form.

Can the female form exist without sex? is a body female if the stick figure has no skirt. In this series ‘Headlessness” I employ a strategy of bookending a body, using materiality and fiction imposed on female limbs to redirect conversations of a woman's body.

The piece Another Headless Woman uses Therabands, a material used to help strengthen muscle, to replicate many historical depictions of muscle tissue in this classic female resting pose to challenge how we project on the female form.
